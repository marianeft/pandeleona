Pan de Leona is a family-run bakery founded by Nanay Leonida in the quiet town of Leon, Iloilo. It started as a humble stall selling traditional Filipino pandesal, with a mission to create the softest, most flavorful bread using fresh, local ingredients. Named after a lioness to symbolize strength and pride, the bakery expanded its offerings when Nanay’s son, Gabriel, introduced new flavors like ube, cheese, and malunggay. As demand grew, the bakery expanded across the Philippines, while staying true to its values of family, tradition, and community, sourcing ingredients from local farmers and maintaining its connection to Leon.

The Leona Story

Pan de Leona’s mission is to honor the tradition of Filipino baking while constantly innovating to bring new, exciting flavors to life. At its core, the bakery is about more than just bread; it’s about creating a sense of connection, joy, and pride in every bite, from the mountains of Leon to the tables across the nation.

Our Mission

The vision of Pan de Leona is to continue growing its pride—becoming a household name in the Philippines and beyond, while staying true to its roots. It envisions a future where every home has access to the comforting, delicious flavors of Leona Pandesal, and where the bakery serves as a symbol of the warmth, creativity, and resilience of Filipino culture.
